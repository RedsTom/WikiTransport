## What are Anchor Points?

Anchor points are **intermediate control points** on a line segment between two stations. They let you shape the path — for example, to route a line around an obstacle or create a specific curve.

Without anchor points, lines go straight from station to station. With anchors, you can bend the line.

## Adding an Anchor

**Method 1 — Right-click:** Right-click on a line segment and choose <guide-btn icon="anchor">Add anchor</guide-btn>. The anchor will be placed where you clicked.

**Method 2 — Placement mode:** Press `A` or click <guide-btn icon="anchor">Add anchor</guide-btn> in the toolbar. Then click on a line segment to add an anchor there.

## Moving an Anchor

Click and drag an anchor to move it. The line updates in real-time as you drag.

Anchor points can also be **multi-selected** and moved together, just like stations. Use **Ctrl+click** or **Shift+click** to add anchors to the selection, then drag any selected anchor to move them all.

## Deleting an Anchor

Right-click an anchor and choose <guide-btn icon="delete">Delete anchor</guide-btn>, or select the anchor and press `Delete` / `Backspace`.

## Visual Appearance

Anchors appear as small **diamond shapes** along the line. A selected anchor has a black outline. When you hover over an anchor, it turns blue.
