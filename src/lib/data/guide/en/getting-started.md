## What is WikiTransport?

WikiTransport is a tool for creating **schematic transit maps** — the kind you see on metro, tram, and bus networks. Unlike geographic maps, schematic maps use straight lines and 45° angles for clarity.

## Interface Overview

The editor has three main areas:

- **Left panel** — browse your lines, transit types, stations, and shared segments. Click the icons on the far left bar to switch between them.
- **Center canvas** — the large working area where you build your map. You can pan by dragging empty space and zoom with the scroll wheel.
- **Right panel** — edit the properties of whatever you have selected (station, line, anchor, etc.).

On the bottom center is the **toolbar** with quick actions for adding stations, anchors, and selecting lines.

## Your First Map in 5 Steps

### 1. Create a transit type

Click <guide-btn icon="category">Line types</guide-btn> in the left bar, then click **New Type**. Give it a name like "Metro" and pick a color and shape.

### 2. Add a line

In <guide-btn icon="map">Overview</guide-btn>, click **+ Add Line** next to the Lines section. Choose your transit type, give the line a name (e.g., "Line 1"), and pick a color.

### 3. Add stations

Click <guide-btn icon="add_location">Add station</guide-btn> in the toolbar or press `S`, then click on the canvas to place a station. Give it a name.

Add at least two stations for your line to have a visible route.

### 4. Connect stations to the line

Make sure your line is selected (shown in the toolbar with a colored dot). Right-click a station and choose <guide-btn icon="add_circle">Add to line</guide-btn>. Repeat for each station you want on the line.

### 5. Arrange your map

Drag stations to position them. The line will follow. Use the label direction controls in the right panel to place station names where they fit best.
