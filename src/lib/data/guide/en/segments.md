## What are Segments?

When **two or more lines share the same segment** between two stations, they form a shared segment. Instead of drawing them on top of each other, WikiTransport offsets them so each line is visible side by side.

Shared segments are detected automatically — you don't need to create them manually.

## Adjusting Line Order in a Shared Segment

When lines share a segment, you can control which line appears on top:

1. Open <guide-btn icon="linear_scale">Segments</guide-btn> in the left bar
2. Click on a shared segment in the list
3. In the right panel, drag the lines to reorder them

The top line in the list is drawn on the outside (furthest from the center), and the bottom line is drawn on the inside.

## Visual Indicators

- **Hover** over a shared segment on the canvas — it highlights in blue to show the shared path
- **Select** a shared segment to see and edit its properties in the right panel

## Corner Rounding

When a line changes direction at a station, it creates a corner. You can adjust how rounded these corners are:

1. Hover over a junction point — a circle indicator appears
2. Click to select it
3. In the right panel (Corners section), use the slider to adjust the **corner radius**

Smaller values = sharper corners. Larger values = rounder curves.
